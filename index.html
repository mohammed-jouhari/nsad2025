Scope

AI-Driven Network Security and Anomaly Detection (NSAD) is an online workshop of EngiTek 2025. The event is intended to represent a major forum for researchers, academicians, professionals, and students worldwide to present their latest research results and exchange ideas in this exciting field. EngiTek 2025 is organized by the Faculty of Engineering (FoE) at Jordan University of Science and Technology (JUST) in collaboration with InnovCOM Lab of SupCOM in Tunisia, and is technically co-sponsored by the IEEE Jordan Section. Proceedings will be published by IEEE Xplore.

Co-Located with the 2025 International Conference on Engineering and Computing Technologies (EngiTek 2025).

Topics of Interest

  • Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning Techniques for Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS)
  • Anomaly Detection in IoT, Edge, and 6G Network Environments
  • Lightweight and Energy-Efficient AI Models for Resource-Constrained Devices
  • Federated and Distributed Learning Approaches for Network Security
  • Deep Learning and Reinforcement Learning for Advanced Cyberattack Detection
  • Blockchain and Trust Mechanisms Integrated with AI-Based IDS
  • Digital Twins and Simulation Frameworks for Security and Anomaly Analysis
  • Privacy-Preserving and Explainable AI for Secure Network Monitoring
  • Benchmark Datasets, Evaluation Metrics, and Reproducibility in IDS Research
  • Applications of AI-Driven IDS in Critical Infrastructures and Smart Cities

Submission Guidelines

  • Only full paper submissions are accepted. Abstracts or extended summaries will not be considered.
  • Submitted manuscripts should not exceed six pages, must use Times New Roman size 10 font, double columns, and single line spacing.
  • Submissions will undergo rigorous peer review, similarity checks, and AI generation checks. Manuscripts with high similarity or Gen-AI scores will be rejected.
  • Acceptance decisions are based on importance, originality, clarity, and contribution.
